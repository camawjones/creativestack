# Utilisation Framework

Creative agency benchmarks. Not consulting numbers, not dev shop numbers. The
maths is the same, but the meaning is different — creative work degrades
faster under pressure than execution work because it requires unobstructed
thinking time, not just available hours.

## The bands

| Band | Status | What it means | Sustainable for |
|---|---|---|---|
| <60% | **Under-utilised** | Capacity available. Good for pitch teams, bench strength, learning, internal. | Indefinitely (but watch for boredom / under-stimulation) |
| 60-70% | **Healthy** | Room for reactive work, internal projects, thinking time. Quality compounds. | Indefinitely |
| 70-80% | **Optimal** | Productive and sustainable. Target for most creative roles. | Indefinitely |
| 80-85% | **Stretched** | Manageable. No buffer for surprises. | 4-8 weeks |
| 85-90% | **At risk** | Creative quality drops measurably. Work gets executed, not elevated. | 2-4 weeks |
| 90%+ | **Burnout risk** | Unsustainable. Expect quality issues, sick days, attrition within 4-8 weeks. | <2 weeks |

## Why creative quality drops above 85%

This is the single most important insight to communicate to leadership. The
difference between 80% and 90% looks like 10% on a spreadsheet but feels like
the difference between **good work** and **survival mode** on the studio floor.

Three things break above 85%:

1. **Exploration time disappears.** The 15% buffer is where designers try the
   second and third route. Without it, the first idea ships. The first idea
   is rarely the best idea.
2. **Context-switching tax compounds.** People at 90% are usually on 3+ projects.
   Every switch is a 15-20 minute warm-up. Five switches a day = an hour of
   unbillable cognitive friction.
3. **Mentoring stops.** Senior creatives drop the unpaid coaching that keeps
   juniors growing and the studio's craft level rising. This is invisible
   for 6 weeks then catastrophic for 12 months.

## Role-specific targets

Override the global target where the brain has `seniority` set:

| Seniority | Target | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Junior / mid (executor) | 75-80% | Mostly billable production work. Higher utilisation tolerable. |
| Senior (creator) | 70-75% | Exploration time matters. Quality drops fastest in this band. |
| Lead / CD (director) | 60-70% | Half their value is reviewing other people's work and guiding direction. Booking them at 80% means they stop reviewing. |
| Strategist | 60-70% | Strategy needs reading, talking, thinking — none of which logs as billable hours. |
| Producer / PM | 80-85% | Coordination work scales differently. Higher tolerance. |

If `seniority` isn't set in `team.md`, fall back to the global target the user
selected (or 75% default).

## Creative quality risk scoring

For each person above target, score the creative quality risk:

| Risk | Trigger | Symptom |
|---|---|---|
| **Low** | At target ±5% | None expected |
| **Medium** | 5-10% above target | Mild — first ideas shipping, less iteration |
| **High** | 10-15% above target | Visible — work feels safe, not exceptional |
| **Critical** | 15%+ above target, OR target + split across 4+ projects | Work executes but doesn't elevate. Client pushback likely. |

Multipliers (escalate one band each):
- Person is unsupported (only senior on the project)
- Person is on a high-stakes project (pitch, big launch, important client)
- Person had no break since last crunch (cumulative fatigue)
- `learnings.md` § Capacity has prior instances of this person blowing through allocations

## Junior + senior asymmetry

A junior at 95% and a senior at 65% is **not the same problem** as a senior at
95% and a junior at 65%. The skill should treat these differently:

- **Overbooked junior, free senior:** Senior probably can't take on the junior's
  execution work (different skill, different speed) but **can absorb the review
  burden** that's hidden in the junior's allocation. Recommend the senior take
  on direction so the junior can focus on execution.
- **Overbooked senior, free junior:** Junior usually can't substitute for the
  senior's creative judgement. The right play is offloading the senior's
  *coordination and execution* to the junior, freeing the senior for the
  judgement work only they can do.

This logic lives in `rebalancing-patterns.md` — but the framework here is what
it depends on.
