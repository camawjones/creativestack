# Brief Sharpener Rubric

10 dimensions aligned with `creative-brief`'s rubric so the two skills
share a language. The creative-brief rubric scores briefs *as they are
built*; the sharpener rubric scores *briefs as they arrive* — usually
from a client, usually messy, usually missing the things creatives
need most.

Traffic light: Green 7+, Amber 4-6, Red 1-3.
Any Red dimension **blocks** the brief from being ready for the team,
regardless of average.

---

## The 10 dimensions

### 1. Tension / Why Now

**What it measures:** Does the brief explain *why this work exists
now*? Is there a gap between where the brand is and where it needs
to be, or is the brief just a list of deliverables?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Clear single-sentence tension with two sides, specific to this brand, time-bound |
| 7-8 | Tension is implied and recoverable but not stated |
| 4-6 | Tension is missing but the brief describes a business situation that implies one |
| 1-3 | Pure deliverable spec — no reason, no situation, no "why now" |

**Sharpen move:** If 4-6, extract the implied tension and reflect
it back as a question. If 1-3, flag as a project killer in Tier 1.

---

### 2. Real Ask vs Stated Ask

**What it measures:** Is the real ask separable from the stated ask?
Clients almost always brief at the deliverable level ("we need a
rebrand") when the real ask is a business outcome ("we're losing
price power on the shelf"). The sharpener's job is to find the
hidden brief.

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Real ask stated separately from stated ask; 3+ "whys" deep |
| 7-8 | Real ask is partly visible; one more why would surface it |
| 4-6 | Brief is entirely at deliverable level but context suggests a real ask |
| 1-3 | Brief is 100% deliverable spec with no business context at all |

**Sharpen move:** Apply the 5-whys from `question-laddering.md` to
extract the real ask. Reflect it back as "I think the real ask is
X — can you confirm?" rather than silently replacing.

---

### 3. Audience Depth

**What it measures:** Does the audience description pass the 4-layer
check — behaviour, relationship, context, desire — or is it pure
demographics?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | All 4 layers present; includes a non-obvious insight |
| 7-8 | 3 layers; insight is thin or missing |
| 4-6 | 2 layers; mostly demographic |
| 1-3 | "Our customers" or "millennials 25-45" |

**Sharpen move:** For each missing layer, generate one specific
question. The team needs at minimum Layer 1 (behaviour) and Layer 4
(desire) before they can start.

---

### 4. Success Criteria

**What it measures:** How will we know this worked? Is there a
measurable or agreed-subjective criterion per objective?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Each objective has a measurable or agreed-subjective criterion |
| 7-8 | Most objectives have criteria; one or two are vague |
| 4-6 | Success is vague ("modernise", "feel fresh", "stand out") |
| 1-3 | "We'll know it when we see it" |

**Sharpen move:** For Red, this is always a Tier 1 question.
Creative work with no success definition is unapprovable work.

---

### 5. Deliverables & Scope Precision

**What it measures:** Are the deliverables specific enough to scope,
price, and hand to production?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Specific formats, quantities, specs, must-have vs nice-to-have |
| 7-8 | Clear list but some specs are vague |
| 4-6 | High-level list ("a campaign", "a website") |
| 1-3 | Deliverables not defined — just "creative work" |

**Sharpen move:** If 4-6, ask for the specific items the client
expects on day 1, day 30, day 60. If 1-3, flag that no scope is
possible without a deliverable list.

---

### 6. Constraints & Mandatories

**What it measures:** Budget range, timeline, brand guidelines,
legal, accessibility, technical — the things the work must not
violate.

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Budget, timeline, key dates, brand guide, legal all stated |
| 7-8 | Most present; one category missing |
| 4-6 | Two or more categories missing |
| 1-3 | No constraints stated at all |

**Sharpen move:** Budget absence is almost always deliberate, not
an oversight. Ask for a *range* not a number — range is easier for
clients to share. If the brief says "no budget constraints", that
is itself a red flag — see `red-flags.md`.

---

### 7. Stakeholders & Approval Process

**What it measures:** Who decides? Who approves? Who signs off?
Can the team avoid losing days to the wrong reviews?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Clear decision-maker, approvers, review process, FYI list |
| 7-8 | Mostly clear; decision-maker ambiguous in one area |
| 4-6 | Approvers listed but no clear decision-maker |
| 1-3 | "The team" or "the client" — no named people |

**Sharpen move:** If `clients.md` has stakeholder history for this
client, pre-fill what's known and flag what's new or missing.

---

### 8. Creative Space

**What it measures:** How much room does the brief leave for the
creative team? Too prescriptive kills the work; too vague leaves
the team guessing.

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Clear direction with explicit "the team should own X" freedom |
| 7-8 | Direction is clear but constraints edge toward prescriptive |
| 4-6 | Either prescribes the answer or leaves everything open |
| 1-3 | Brief is a solution in disguise ("we need a blue logo with a lion") |

**Sharpen move:** For prescriptive briefs, ask "what problem is the
prescribed solution solving?" and surface it as a Tier 2 question.
For open-ended briefs, ask for a single constraint or reference.

---

### 9. Category & Competitive Context

**What it measures:** Does the brief acknowledge the environment the
work will exist in? Competitors, trends, white space?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Specific competitors, trends, and white space identified |
| 7-8 | Some context; trends thin |
| 4-6 | Competitive mentioned but shallow |
| 1-3 | Brief treats the brand as if it exists in a vacuum |

**Sharpen move:** If upstream `/competitor-audit` or `/trend-report`
ran, cross-reference. If neither ran and the dimension scores 1-6,
offer to run `/source-scrape` for a fast category check.

---

### 10. Internal Consistency (contradictions)

**What it measures:** Does the brief argue with itself? Budget-scope
mismatches, tone contradictions, audience contradictions, timeline
impossibilities.

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | No contradictions; constraints and ambitions align |
| 7-8 | Minor tension ("bold but safe") but recoverable |
| 4-6 | Clear contradiction in one area (usually budget vs scope) |
| 1-3 | Multiple contradictions; the brief is arguing with itself |

**Sharpen move:** Name each contradiction explicitly with
[CONTRADICTION] markers. Don't try to resolve them unilaterally —
surface them as questions for the client.

---

## Aggregate scoring

- **Total:** sum of 10 dimensions (max 100)
- **Average:** total ÷ 10 (the headline 1-10 score)
- **Blocker check:** any dimension scoring 1-3 (Red) blocks readiness

### Readiness states

| Average | Blocker? | State | Next step |
|---|---|---|---|
| 8.0+ | No | **Ready for creative** | Brief is strong. Run `/creativestack:creative-brief` From inputs mode to format it, then timeline-generator |
| 6.5-7.9 | No | **Ready with one follow-up** | Identify the weakest dimension and ask the single sharpest question |
| 5.0-6.4 | No | **Needs tightening** | Send the Tier 1 questions back to the client before the team starts |
| Any | Yes (Red) | **Blocked** | The Red dimension must be resolved before creative work begins |
| <5.0 | Any | **Not a brief yet** | Recommend a client alignment conversation rather than another brief round |

### Scoring discipline

- **Don't grade generously.** If every brief scores 8+, the rubric
  isn't doing its job. Most incoming client briefs score 5-7.
- **Don't grade harshly.** A brief missing one section is not a 1 —
  probably a 4-6 on that dimension.
- **Be specific in notes.** "Too vague" is useless. "Audience is
  'millennials' — needs Layer 2 (relationship to brand) and
  Layer 4 (desire) at minimum" is useful.
- **Confidence labels.** If the brief is very sparse (a few
  sentences), don't score confidently. Add a Low confidence note
  and recommend a longer input or From scratch mode.

## Per-client weighting

If `learnings.md § Feedback` has past project data for this client
showing they consistently under-deliver on specific dimensions, the
sharpener can note:

> **Client context:** Past briefs from this client have failed most
> often on Stakeholders & Approvals (dim 7). Current score: 5.
> Recommend this as the priority question.

This is an overlay on the rubric, not a change to scoring. Same 10
dimensions, adjusted prioritisation.

## Rubric ↔ creative-brief alignment

| brief-sharpener dim | creative-brief dim | Relationship |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Tension / Why Now | 1. The Tension | Same |
| 2. Real Ask vs Stated Ask | 2. Real Objective | Same lens, different angle |
| 3. Audience Depth | 4. Target Audience + 5. Insight | Combined — sharpener flags both as one question to client |
| 4. Success Criteria | 3. Success Criteria | Same |
| 5. Deliverables & Scope | 7. Deliverables & Scope | Same |
| 6. Constraints & Mandatories | 8. Constraints & Mandatories | Same |
| 7. Stakeholders & Approvals | 9. Stakeholders & Approvals | Same |
| 8. Creative Space | (implicit in brief) | Sharpener-specific |
| 9. Category & Competitive | 10. Category & Competitive | Same |
| 10. Internal Consistency | (implicit in brief) | Sharpener-specific |
| — | 6. Single-Minded Thought | Not scored by sharpener — that's creative-brief's job to generate |

A brief that scores 7+ on sharpener is typically ready to hand to
`creative-brief` From inputs mode, which will add the Single-Minded
Thought dimension as part of its own rubric.
