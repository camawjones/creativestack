# Brief Completeness Rubric

10 dimensions, each scored 1-10. Traffic light: Green 7+, Amber 4-6, Red 1-3.
The aggregate drives a blocker check — any Red dimension stops the brief
from being ready, regardless of total score.

This rubric is tougher than the previous 7-dimension version because it
adds the four pressure points from `tension-and-insight.md` as scored
dimensions. A brief that aces deliverables but has no tension should score
low, because the creative team can't work from it.

---

## The 10 dimensions

### 1. The Tension (new)

**What it measures:** Is there a clear gap between where the brand is and
where it needs to be? Does the brief explain why this project exists now?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Sharp single-sentence tension with two sides, specific to this brand, explains why now |
| 7-8 | Clear tension but could be sharper; survives pressure tests |
| 4-6 | Implied tension but not stated; or stated but too generic |
| 1-3 | No tension at all — the brief describes a deliverable, not a reason |

**What good looks like:** "Nike Air was built for performance but lives in
a culture that cares more about meaning than specs — performance authority
is no longer enough."

**Red flag:** "The brand needs a refresh." (description, not tension)

---

### 2. Real Objective (was: Objectives)

**What it measures:** Is there a stated objective separated from the
stated deliverable? Can the team tell why the work matters?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Stated and real objective both clear; real objective is 3+ "whys" deep |
| 7-8 | Real objective surfaced; could be sharper |
| 4-6 | Stated objective present but no real objective separation |
| 1-3 | Objective is actually a deliverable in disguise |

**What good looks like:** Stated: "Redesign the website." Real: "Signal
active product investment so customers stop defecting on perception grounds."

**Red flag:** "Build a new identity." (this is what, not why)

---

### 3. Success Criteria

**What it measures:** How will we know this worked in 6 months? Is there at
least one measurable or agreed-subjective criterion per objective?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Each objective has a measurable or agreed-subjective criterion |
| 7-8 | Most objectives have criteria; one or two are vague |
| 4-6 | Success vaguely defined ("modernise", "improve") |
| 1-3 | No success criteria at all |

**What good looks like:** "Retention on the 'actively investing' attribute
in quarterly NPS rises by 10 points in Q3."

**Red flag:** "We'll know when we see it."

---

### 4. Target Audience (sharpened)

**What it measures:** Does the audience description pass the 4-layer check
from `tension-and-insight.md` (behaviour, relationship, context, desire)?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | All 4 layers present; includes a non-obvious insight |
| 7-8 | 3 layers present; insight is thin or missing |
| 4-6 | 2 layers present; mostly demographic |
| 1-3 | Pure demographics ("millennials", "women 35-54") |

**What good looks like:** "People who've run 3+ marathons and are now
training for their first ultra, mid-scroll on Instagram, wanting to feel
like the brand understands that ambition doesn't need external validation."

**Red flag:** "Our customers."

---

### 5. Insight (new — separated from Audience)

**What it measures:** Is there a non-obvious truth about the audience that
unlocks the creative? Not data, not demographics — an actual insight.

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | A specific, non-obvious insight that changes what the team would make |
| 7-8 | An insight is present but slightly generic or could be sharper |
| 4-6 | Observation or data dressed up as insight |
| 1-3 | No insight — just demographics or statistics |

**What good looks like:** "They're checking the app in the morning to feel
prepared, not to learn something new — the morning check-in is a ritual,
not a task."

**Red flag:** "Our audience uses social media heavily." (data, not insight)

---

### 6. Single-Minded Thought (new)

**What it measures:** Is there one sentence the whole team can rally
around? Does it pass the pressure tests from `tension-and-insight.md`?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | A sharp single-sentence thought that passes all 4 pressure tests |
| 7-8 | A thought is present but could fail 1-2 pressure tests |
| 4-6 | Candidate thought flagged as needing client validation |
| 1-3 | No thought at all; the brief is a list of deliverables |

**What good looks like:** "To runners training for their first ultra, Nike
Air is the shoe brand that honours their ambition without asking them to
prove it yet."

**Red flag:** "A campaign that speaks to runners." (describes the output,
not the thought)

---

### 7. Deliverables & Scope

**What it measures:** Are the deliverables specific enough to scope, price,
and hand off to production?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Specific formats, quantities, specs, and priority (must-have vs nice-to-have) |
| 7-8 | Clear list but some specs are vague |
| 4-6 | High-level list ("a campaign", "a website") |
| 1-3 | Deliverables not defined — just "creative work" |

**What good looks like:** "One 30s hero film, three 15s cutdowns (9:16, 1:1,
16:9), four key visuals (social + OOH), six social posts, a landing page,
and a press kit. Must-haves: hero film and KVs. Nice-to-haves: cutdowns."

**Red flag:** "The usual campaign stuff."

---

### 8. Constraints & Mandatories

**What it measures:** Budget, timeline, legal, technical, brand guidelines —
the things the work must not violate.

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Budget, timeline, key dates, brand guidelines, legal all stated |
| 7-8 | Most present but one category missing |
| 4-6 | Two or more categories missing |
| 1-3 | No constraints stated at all |

**What good looks like:** Budget range, launch date, brand guide link,
accessibility requirements, legal sign-off process, approval layers.

**Red flag:** "We don't want to constrain creativity." (this attitude
always causes downstream problems)

---

### 9. Stakeholders & Approvals

**What it measures:** Who decides? Who approves? Who signs off? Is the
approval process clear enough that the team won't lose days to the wrong
reviews?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Clear decision-maker, clear approvers, clear review process, clear FYI list |
| 7-8 | Mostly clear but the decision-maker is ambiguous in one area |
| 4-6 | Approvers listed but no clear decision-maker |
| 1-3 | "The team" or "the client" — no named people or roles |

**What good looks like:** "Marcus Liu (CMO) is the decision-maker on
creative. Priya Shah (Brand Director) reviews craft. Legal reviews claims.
Internal launch committee gets FYI only."

**Red flag:** "All stakeholders will review."

---

### 10. Category & Competitive Context

**What it measures:** Does the brief acknowledge the environment the work
will exist in? Trends, competitor moves, white space?

| Score | Criteria |
|---|---|
| 9-10 | Specific competitive context, trends, and white space identified |
| 7-8 | Some competitive context but trends are thin |
| 4-6 | Competitive mentioned but shallow |
| 1-3 | Brief treats the brand as if it exists in a vacuum |

**What good looks like:** 2-3 competitors with specific positioning, one
or two trends reshaping the category, and a clear articulation of what
space is open.

**Red flag:** No competitive mention at all.

---

## Aggregate scoring

- **Total:** Sum of 10 dimensions (max 100)
- **Average:** Total ÷ 10 (for the 1-10 headline score shown in output)
- **Blocker check:** Any dimension scoring 1-3 (Red) blocks brief readiness, regardless of total

### Readiness states

| Aggregate | Blocker present? | State |
|---|---|---|
| 8.0+ | No | **Ready for creative** |
| 6.5-7.9 | No | **Ready with one follow-up** — identify the weakest dimension and address |
| 5.0-6.4 | No | **Needs tightening** — run `/creativestack:brief-sharpener` before briefing the team |
| Any | Yes (Red) | **Blocked** — specific dimension must be resolved before creative begins |
| <5.0 | Any | **Not a brief yet** — schedule an alignment conversation with the client |

The state drives the closing message in the output and the skill chains
section (what to run next).

---

## How the skill uses the rubric

1. **After generating the brief draft**, the skill runs through all 10
   dimensions and scores each 1-10
2. **For each dimension**, the skill writes a one-line note explaining what
   drove the score (what's strong or missing)
3. **The scoring table appears in the output** before the brief itself,
   so the reader sees the assessment before reading the content
4. **Any Red dimension is called out explicitly** as a Blocker in bold
5. **The overall state** drives the closing message

### Scoring discipline

- **Don't grade generously.** A brief that scores 10 on everything is
  either perfect (rare) or the rubric isn't being applied honestly. If
  every brief scores 8+, the rubric isn't doing its job.
- **Don't grade harshly.** A brief missing one section is not a 1 — it's
  probably a 4-6 on that dimension.
- **Be specific in the notes.** "Too vague" is not a useful note. "Audience
  is 'millennials' — needs Layer 2 (relationship to brand) and Layer 4
  (desire) at minimum." is useful.

### Confidence labels

If the brief input is very sparse (a few sentences only), the skill can't
score confidently. In that case:

> **Confidence:** Low — brief inputs are sparse. Scores are directional
> only. Recommend running `/creativestack:creative-brief` again with more
> input, or using **From scratch** mode to walk through the questions.

Don't score confidently off thin inputs.

## Per-client rubric weighting (optional)

If `learnings.md` has past project feedback that shows a particular
dimension matters more for this client (e.g. "Tate briefs always fail on
stakeholder clarity"), the skill can note this:

> **Client context:** This client's past briefs have failed most often on
> Stakeholder clarity (dimension 9). Current score: 6. Recommend tightening
> this specifically before kickoff.

This is an overlay on the rubric, not a change to the scoring. The
dimensions stay the same; the prioritisation adapts.

---

## When to skip the rubric

Only skip the rubric in two cases:
1. **The user explicitly asks for a brief draft without scoring** (e.g. for
   a rough internal doc)
2. **Brief is very short by design** (e.g. a 1-line phase brief for a
   recurring workstream)

In both cases, the skill should note that scoring was skipped and offer to
apply it later if needed.

Never skip the rubric silently.
