# Job Description Output Formats

Templates for the JD itself, the inclusivity rubric, and the posting channel
recommendations. Generated by `/creativestack:job-description`.

---

## Full Job Description

```markdown
# {Role Title}
**{Agency Name}** | {Location / Remote Policy} | {Employment Type}
**Salary:** {range — always a band, never a single number}
**Reports to:** {role}
**Application deadline:** {date or "rolling"}

## About Us
{2-3 sentences pulled from profile.md. Specific to *this* studio, not generic agency
filler. The first sentence should be something a competitor couldn't honestly say.
Cut anything that could appear in any other agency's "about us".}

## The Role
{Plain-language description of what this person will actually do day-to-day. Frame
around the impact and the work, not corporate competencies. Two short paragraphs is
plenty. Avoid: "exciting opportunity", "fast-paced", "wear many hats", "rockstar",
"ninja", "passionate".}

## What You'll Be Doing
- {Specific responsibility — concrete, real, scoped to a normal week}
- {Responsibility}
- {Responsibility}
- {Responsibility}
- {Responsibility — 5-7 max. More than that and it stops being a role description
  and starts being a wishlist.}

## What We're Looking For

**You'll need:**
- {Genuine requirement — skill, experience, or quality the role can't function without}
- {Genuine requirement}
- {Genuine requirement — keep this list to 4-6 items max. Every item is something a
  candidate has to clear; the longer the list, the more strong candidates self-select
  out (research consistently shows this hits women and underrepresented candidates
  hardest).}

**It'd be great if you also had:**
- {Nice-to-have — clearly separated from must-haves}
- {Nice-to-have}
- {Nice-to-have}

**You don't need:**
- {Optional but powerful section. List things candidates often assume they need but
  don't — e.g., "A degree in design", "Agency experience", "5 years in this exact
  tool". Lowers the self-selection barrier for career changers and underrepresented
  candidates.}

## What We Offer
- {Specific benefit — not "competitive salary" or "great culture". Number, name,
  or concrete commitment.}
- {Benefit — e.g., "32-day holiday + bank holidays", "Annual learning budget of
  £2,000", "Hybrid: 2 days in our Hackney studio, 3 from home"}
- {Benefit}
- {Benefit}

## How to Apply
{Clear instructions: what to send, where to send it, what the process looks like,
how long it takes. Be specific about portfolio expectations and whether there's a
take-home task. State explicitly if take-home tasks are paid (they should be).}

**The process:**
1. {Step — e.g., "Application review (1-2 weeks)"}
2. {Step — e.g., "30-min intro call with {name}"}
3. {Step — e.g., "Portfolio walkthrough + craft conversation (60 min)"}
4. {Step — e.g., "Paid half-day task (£{amount}) — only if it adds genuine signal"}
5. {Step — e.g., "Final conversation with {name}"}

**Accessibility & accommodations:**
{One short paragraph stating the studio's commitment to accessible hiring and how
candidates can request adjustments. Name a real person to contact, not a generic
inbox.}

**We welcome applications from people who don't tick every box.** If you're excited
about the role and think you could do it well, please apply.
```

## Inclusivity Score (internal — strip before posting)

specific improvements for any dimension below 8.

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|

## Red Flag Detection (internal — strip before posting)

Common problems in creative hiring. Surface only the flags that apply.

| Flag | Severity | Detail |
|------|----------|--------|
| **Unicorn role** | High / Medium | {e.g., "This describes a designer + developer + strategist. Three jobs in one. Decide which is the primary discipline."} |
| **Salary/seniority mismatch** | High / Medium | {e.g., "Senior responsibilities at mid-weight salary. You'll attract mid-weights or frustrate seniors."} |
| **Excessive requirements** | Medium | {e.g., "11 must-have requirements. The strongest candidates will self-select out."} |
| **Vague role** | Medium | {e.g., "Day-to-day is unclear. 'Support the team' could mean anything from filing to leading projects."} |
| **Unrealistic experience** | High | {e.g., "'5+ years Figma experience' — Figma was only adopted by most agencies in 2020."} |
| **Missing growth path** | Low | {e.g., "No mention of where this role leads. Important signal for junior and mid-weight hires."} |
| **Hidden cost to apply** | High | {e.g., "Requires 3-day unpaid task before first interview. This filters for financial security, not talent."} |
| **Buzzword bingo** | Medium | {e.g., "'Rockstar', 'ninja', 'passionate', 'fast-paced', 'wear many hats' — every one of these dilutes the JD."} |
| **Missing accessibility statement** | Medium | {No mention of accommodations or accessible hiring} |
| **No salary band** | Medium | {Reduces applications ~30%, disproportionately deters women and underrepresented candidates} |

If no red flags apply, say so explicitly.

## Posting Channels

After generating the JD, recommend 3-5 channels to post on, weighted by role.
The goal is reach *and* candidate diversity — don't just default to LinkedIn.

### Cross-discipline (any creative role)
- **Working Not Working** — strong design, art direction, motion, copy talent
- **The Dots** — UK creative network, especially good for accounts/strategy/design
- **LinkedIn** — broad reach but expensive at scale; use with tailored copy
- **People of Craft** — diversity-focused job board for design and creative roles
- **Creative Equals Job Board** — UK, focused on equality in creative industries
- **Where Are The Black Designers Slack** — for designer hires explicitly
- **Underrepresented in Tech / Pocstock community** — for dev and design roles

### Designer / Art Director / Motion
- Working Not Working, It's Nice That, AIGA Design Jobs, Coroflot, Behance Jobs
- Site-specific: Brand New Jobs (brand design), Motionographer (motion)

### Copywriter / Strategist
- The Dots, Copy Hackers, Creative Brief jobs board, D&AD New Blood network

### Account Manager / Producer
- The Dots, LinkedIn, Major Players, Aquent, Profero/Oliver job networks

### Developer
- WeWorkRemotely, Hacker News "Who is Hiring", Stack Overflow Jobs (where active),
  Working Not Working (creative-coded roles), Awwwards Jobs

### Diversity-first boards (always include at least one)
- People of Craft, Where Are The Black Designers, Creative Equals, Pocstock,
  Hire Tech Ladies, Underrepresented in Tech

### Channels to be skeptical of
- Indeed and ZipRecruiter for senior creative roles — high noise, low signal
- Generic "post once everywhere" tools — they often produce volume without quality

State posting recommendations as a short bulleted list with 1 line each on why.
Don't dump the whole reference into the conversation.

## team.md write-back

After generating, write a planned-hire entry to `~/.creativestack/team.md` under a
`## Planned Hires` section. Schema:

```markdown
### {Role Title}
- **Level:** {junior / mid / senior / lead}
- **Status:** Open / Drafted JD / Posted / Interviewing / Hired / Closed
- **Type:** Permanent / Freelance / Fixed-term
- **Salary band:** {range}
- **Posted:** {date or empty}
- **Notes:** {any constraints, why hiring, what the gap is}
- **Reports to:** {role}
```

If the role is filled later, the user can move the entry from `## Planned Hires` to
`## Team` manually, or run `/creativestack:setup-agency` to refresh team data.
