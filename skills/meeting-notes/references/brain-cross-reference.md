# Brain Cross-Reference

This is the operational core of meeting-notes. It's what makes the skill
worth the awkward handover from a recording tool.

The transcript contains what was said. The brain contains what was decided
before, what was promised in the brief, what the methodology says, who's
overbooked, how much things actually cost, and what this client has said
in past meetings. **Cross-referencing the two is the unique value.**

The output of this cross-reference becomes a first-class section: **"What Only
the Brain Caught"**.

---

## What to read from the brain

For every meeting-notes run, read these in order. If a file doesn't exist,
skip it silently — the skill works degraded but not broken.

| Source | Purpose | Cross-reference target |
|---|---|---|
| `projects/{slug}.md` | Project state — current phase, open decisions, risks, engagement health, recent activity | New decisions match open decisions? Recurring risks? |
| `projects/{slug}-meetings.md` | All past meetings on this project | Recurring scope items? Action completion rates? Sentiment trajectory? |
| Brief content (search for `## Brief` or read from project state) | Original brief deliverables, constraints, success criteria | Decisions contradict brief? Scope additions vs brief? |
| `methodology.md` | Agency's standard process steps | Decisions skip a methodology step? |
| `rate-card.md` | Internal cost rates by role | Scope changes priced in £ |
| `team.md` | Team capacity, day rates, leave patterns | Actions assigned to overbooked or absent people? |
| `clients.md` | Client relationship history | Recurring client behaviour? Ongoing tensions? |
| `learnings.md § Client Patterns` | Per-client communication patterns saved from past meetings | Did this meeting confirm or extend a known pattern? |
| `learnings.md § Pitching` | If this is a pitch meeting | Past pitch learnings for this sector/type |
| `tone-of-voice.md` | Agency voice | Used for client-facing output, not cross-reference |

## The 7 cross-reference checks

For each meeting, run these checks against the extracted signals.

### 1. Brief contradiction check

For each decision and scope change in the meeting, search the brief content for
contradicting language.

**Trigger:** Meeting decision contradicts a deliverable, constraint, or success
criterion explicitly in the brief.

**Output format:**
> ⚠️ **Brief contradiction:** The client agreed today to "lock typography by
> Friday." The brief (2026-03-12) lists typography as a *Phase 2* deliverable,
> not Phase 1. Confirm whether this is a brief update or a misalignment.

If the brief was generated by `/creative-brief` earlier in the session, this
check is automatic. If only the project state file is available, check its
"Brief Summary" or "Open Decisions" sections.

### 2. Methodology violation check

For each decision, check if it skips a step the agency's methodology requires.

**Trigger:** Decision proceeds past a step that `methodology.md` describes as
mandatory or sequential (e.g. "tissue session before concept", "validation
phase before production").

**Output format:**
> ⚠️ **Methodology gap:** The team agreed to skip the validation phase and go
> straight to production. Your methodology.md lists validation as a required
> step before production. Was this intentional?

This is a soft flag — the agency may have decided to skip for good reason.
Don't frame it as wrong, frame it as worth confirming.

### 3. Scope change cost check

For each scope change (addition, expansion, scope shift), compute the rough
cost using `rate-card.md`.

**Math:**
- Estimate the hours required (use the range, not a point — e.g. 16-24h)
- Look up the role(s) most likely to do the work in `rate-card.md`
- Multiply: `hours × (internal_cost_day ÷ 8)`
- Output as a range: "£1,400-2,100"

**Output format:**
> 💰 **Scope change priced:** "Add a social rollout" — estimated 16-24h of mid
> designer time = **£700-1,050 internal cost**. At your billing rate, this
> would normally bill at £1,500-2,250. Quote the client before agreeing.

If `rate-card.md` doesn't exist, skip the £ figure but still flag the scope
change with hours estimate and a note: "Run /creativestack:project-profitability
in Setup rates mode to enable cost estimation."

### 4. Action capacity check

For each action with an owner, check `team.md` (with `seniority` and
`contracted_hours` fields if available) and any recent
`/creativestack:resource-conflict` output for capacity warnings.

**Trigger:** Action assigned to someone who is at >85% utilisation per recent
data, OR on leave during the action's deadline period.

**Output format:**
> ⚠️ **Capacity flag:** Sarah took the action to deliver concepts by Friday,
> but per resource-conflict last run, she's at 92% utilisation. This action
> is at risk before it starts.

If no recent resource-conflict run, fall back to `team.md § leave_pattern`
checks only.

### 5. Recurring scope check

Search `projects/{slug}-meetings.md` for past mentions of the same scope item.

**Trigger:** Scope item or change has been mentioned in 2+ previous meetings.

**Output format:**
> 🔁 **Recurring item:** The "additional landing page" has now come up in 3
> meetings (2026-03-04, 2026-03-22, 2026-04-08). Past meetings discussed it
> but no decision was made. This is becoming scope creep — either add it
> formally with a fee adjustment or close it out.

Recurring items are the highest-leverage finding from cross-reference. They
reveal scope creep patterns no single meeting would expose.

### 6. Action completion check

Read the previous meeting's actions from `projects/{slug}-meetings.md`. For each,
check whether it's been mentioned in this meeting as completed, in progress, or
not at all.

**Output format:**
> 📋 **Last meeting's actions (3 of 4 closed):**
> - ✅ Sarah delivered the wireframes
> - ✅ Mike sent the cost estimate
> - ✅ Client confirmed launch date
> - ❌ Marcus to share competitor research — **5 days overdue, not mentioned today**

Open actions from past meetings should be surfaced even if the current meeting
ignored them. They're the easiest things to forget and the most damaging.

### 7. Client pattern check

Read `learnings.md § Client Patterns` and search for patterns associated with
the people in this meeting.

**Trigger:** A phrase or behaviour in the transcript matches a known pattern
for this client or person.

**Output format:**
> 🔍 **Pattern caught:** Marcus said "we'll think about it" — per learnings.md,
> Marcus has used this phrase 2 times before, both preceding a no. **Treat as
> soft no, not a deferral.** Worth a follow-up call this week.

This is the killer brain feature. No recording tool can do this.

If a pattern is detected with high confidence and is NEW (not yet in
learnings.md), the skill should offer to save it during the compounding loop.

## "What Only the Brain Caught" — output section

These 7 checks generate the most valuable section in the output. Format it
as a standalone block, prominently placed, **before** the standard decisions
and actions sections.

```markdown
## ⚡ What Only the Brain Caught

{Lead with the most actionable insight. If there's a high-confidence client
pattern detection, lead with that. Otherwise lead with the most expensive
or risky finding.}

⚠️ **Brief contradiction:** {as above}

🔍 **Pattern caught:** {as above}

💰 **Scope change priced:** {as above}

📋 **Last meeting's actions (3 of 4 closed):** {as above}

🔁 **Recurring item:** {as above}

⚠️ **Methodology gap:** {as above}

⚠️ **Capacity flag:** {as above}

{Skip any check that found nothing. Don't pad with "no contradictions detected"
— silence is the right answer when there's nothing to flag.}
```

### Section ordering

Order findings by **action urgency**, not by check number:

1. Brief contradictions and pattern catches that imply imminent action
2. Scope cost surprises (the user needs to act before agreeing)
3. Capacity flags (the action is at risk now)
4. Action completion failures (overdue items)
5. Recurring patterns (scope creep watch)
6. Methodology gaps (worth confirming, not urgent)

### Confidence labels

Each finding should be implicitly or explicitly confidence-labelled:

- **High** confidence: clear contradiction, exact phrase match, rate card available, clear data
- **Medium**: inferred contradiction, similar phrase, estimated rate, partial data
- **Low**: speculative, single past instance, no rate card, weak pattern

Mark Low and Medium confidence findings with hedging language: "may", "appears to",
"worth confirming". Mark High confidence findings as direct statements.

### When to skip the section entirely

If all 7 checks return nothing, skip the section. Write at the bottom of the
output instead:

> *Brain cross-reference: no flags. The brain checked decisions against the
> brief, methodology, project state, past meetings, and client patterns.
> Nothing to escalate.*

This is honest reporting. Don't fabricate insights to fill the section.

---

## What this section is NOT for

- General observations about the meeting (those go in Tone & Sentiment)
- Predictions about future meetings (those belong in Patterns mode)
- Generic AI insights ("communication is important") — only flag specific,
  brain-evidenced findings
- Padding with "no issues detected" lines for every check that returned empty
- Speculation framed as fact

The section should feel like a senior PM or producer reviewing the notes
**with the project history in front of them** — because functionally that's
what the brain enables.
