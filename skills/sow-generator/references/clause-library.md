# Clause Starting Points

Read this file when:
- A user has no saved clauses and is in setup mode
- A user is in "manage clauses → suggest missing" mode
- A user asks "what clauses should I have?"

**These are starting points, not legal templates.** They're worded for common agency situations and need legal review before use. When a user wants to save one to their library, paste it as-is and let them edit before saving — never silently change the wording.

## Intellectual Property

### IP transfer on final payment (most common)
> All intellectual property rights in the final deliverables transfer to the Client upon receipt of final payment in full. Until then, all rights remain with the Provider. The Provider retains the right to display the work in its portfolio, case studies, and marketing materials, with reasonable confidentiality respected. Third-party assets (stock imagery, fonts, software) are licensed separately and remain subject to their own terms.

### IP transfer with usage license-back
> All intellectual property rights in the final deliverables transfer to the Client upon final payment. The Client grants the Provider a perpetual, non-exclusive license to use the work for portfolio, case study, awards entry, and marketing purposes.

### Provider retains IP, Client gets a license
> The Provider retains all intellectual property rights in the deliverables. The Client receives a perpetual, worldwide, non-exclusive license to use the deliverables for the purposes described in this SOW.

## Confidentiality

### Mutual NDA (standard)
> Both parties agree to treat all non-public information disclosed during this engagement as confidential. Neither party will disclose such information to third parties without prior written consent, except to employees, contractors, and advisors who need to know and are bound by similar confidentiality obligations. This obligation survives termination of this SOW for {duration, e.g. 2 years}.

### Mutual NDA with exclusions
> {Standard mutual NDA above} ... Confidential information does not include information that: (a) was already known to the receiving party; (b) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the receiving party; (c) is independently developed without use of confidential information; or (d) is required to be disclosed by law, provided the disclosing party is given reasonable notice.

## Termination

### Notice-based termination
> Either party may terminate this SOW with {n} days' written notice. Upon termination, the Client agrees to pay for all work completed up to the termination date, plus any non-cancellable third-party costs. The Provider will deliver all completed work upon receipt of final payment.

### For-cause termination
> Either party may terminate this SOW immediately upon written notice if the other party materially breaches its obligations and fails to cure such breach within {n} days of written notice.

## Kill Fee

### Standard kill fee
> If the Client cancels this project after execution but before completion, a kill fee will apply: 25% of the total project fee if cancelled during the first phase, 50% if cancelled mid-project, and 75% if cancelled in the final phase. Kill fees are in addition to payment for all work completed to date.

### Simple kill fee
> If the Client cancels this project after execution, the Client agrees to pay 50% of the remaining unbilled fee as a kill fee, in addition to all work completed to date.

## Rush Surcharge

> Work requested with a turnaround of less than {n} business days from request to delivery is subject to a rush surcharge of {%} of the standard fee. Rush requests are accommodated subject to the Provider's availability and are not guaranteed.

## AI Usage Policy

### Provider uses AI tools (transparency)
> The Provider may use AI tools to support the creative process (research, ideation, asset generation, copy drafting, code scaffolding). All AI-generated content used in deliverables is reviewed, edited, and approved by human creative professionals. The Provider does not submit Client confidential information to public AI services without explicit written consent.

### Provider does not use AI on this project
> The Provider will not use generative AI tools to produce deliverables for this project. All creative work will be human-generated.

### Client wants to know what models are used
> The Provider will disclose, on request, which AI tools and models were used in the production of deliverables. The Provider will not train AI models on Client confidential information or deliverables.

## Force Majeure

> Neither party shall be liable for delay or failure to perform any obligation under this SOW where such delay or failure results from circumstances beyond reasonable control, including but not limited to natural disasters, war, civil unrest, pandemic, government action, internet outages, or third-party service failures. The affected party will notify the other party as soon as reasonably possible and the timeline will be adjusted accordingly.

## Indemnification

### Mutual indemnification
> Each party agrees to indemnify and hold the other harmless from any third-party claims arising from the indemnifying party's negligence, breach of this SOW, or infringement of third-party intellectual property rights. Total liability under this clause is capped at the total fees paid under this SOW.

## Limitation of Liability

> The Provider's total liability under this SOW, regardless of the cause of action, is limited to the total fees paid by the Client under this SOW. Neither party shall be liable for indirect, consequential, incidental, or punitive damages.

## Subcontractor Disclosure

> The Provider may engage subcontractors or freelancers to deliver portions of this work. The Provider remains responsible for the quality of all work delivered. The Provider will ensure all subcontractors are bound by equivalent confidentiality obligations.

## Data Handling (GDPR / privacy-aware)

> Where the Provider processes personal data on behalf of the Client, the Provider will: (a) process such data only on documented instructions from the Client; (b) ensure persons authorised to process the data are bound by confidentiality; (c) implement appropriate technical and organisational security measures; (d) assist the Client with data subject requests; and (e) delete or return personal data at the end of the engagement. A separate Data Processing Agreement may be required for projects involving significant personal data processing.

## Sustainability Commitments (optional)

> The Provider commits to: prioritising digital deliverables over print where possible; using FSC-certified materials when print is required; offsetting carbon emissions for any project travel; and disclosing the environmental considerations of recommended production methods.

## Accessibility Commitments (optional)

> All digital deliverables will meet WCAG {2.1 / 2.2} {Level AA} accessibility standards unless otherwise scoped. The Provider will conduct accessibility review as part of QA. Additional accessibility testing (assistive technology user testing, full audit) is available as an add-on.

## DEI Rider (optional)

> The Provider commits to: representing diverse perspectives in casting, photography, and illustration; sourcing from underrepresented suppliers where possible; and reviewing creative output for inclusive language and imagery before delivery.

## Suggested clause checklist

When suggesting clauses a user might be missing, compare their saved library against this list and surface 2-3 high-value gaps:

| Clause | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| IP transfer | Single biggest source of post-project disputes |
| Confidentiality | Standard expectation, often required by client procurement |
| Kill fee | Protects against late-stage cancellation |
| Late payment terms | Sets expectations and gives leverage |
| AI usage policy | Increasingly required by client procurement and legal teams |
| Force majeure | Cheap insurance for events outside both parties' control |
| Subcontractor disclosure | Prevents "who's actually doing this work?" surprises |
| Data handling | Required for GDPR / regulated industries |
| Limitation of liability | Caps exposure on fixed-fee work |

Don't suggest more than 2-3 at a time. Pick the highest-leverage gaps based on the user's project type.
